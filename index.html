Professional Summary

ELA educator and instructional technologist with over 25 years of experience in secondary and post-secondary learning environments, including more than a decade in large urban, Title I, and ESL-majority districts. My work centers on implementing district-provided curriculum with precision, clarity, and accessibility while supporting students’ reading, writing, and analytical skills.

I specialize in internalizing district lessons (including LO/KO, DOLs, instructional notes, and provided slide decks) and adapting them for classroom needs. This includes refining templated or AI-generated questions, adjusting scaffolds, and translating complex academic language into teachable steps without altering district objectives. My background in instructional technology spans LMS design, SCORM development, digital writing tools, and contributions to CLEO, Texas A&M’s open-source synchronous and asynchronous tutoring platform. I maintain familiarity with modern AI models, RAG workflows, and FERPA-aligned use of emerging tools in instructional settings.

Curriculum Implementation & Instructional Adaptation

  • Internalize district-provided NES-aligned curriculum by reviewing daily objectives, DOLs, pacing notes, embedded teacher guidance, and example videos to determine the most effective instructional approach for a given class period.
  • Refine and adapt lesson materials by translating templated or AI-generated questions into clear, grade-appropriate instructional language while maintaining the original LO/KO and DOL requirements.
  • Adjust tasks, scaffolds, and response frames to improve accessibility for ESL learners and struggling readers without altering district expectations or assessment alignment.
  • Implement district slide decks and activity structures by selecting emphasis points, determining pacing, adding clarifying models, and preparing guided practice that maintains instructional fidelity while meeting student needs.
  • Use Multiple Response Strategies (MRS), LSAE differentiation, and targeted questioning to support comprehension and engagement across diverse learning profiles.
  • Integrate instructional technology—including Canvas LMS, PowerSchool, H5P, SCORM content, and digital literacy tools—to enhance student engagement and clarity of teacher-facing materials.
